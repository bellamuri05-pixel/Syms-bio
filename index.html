Martine Syms is a Los Angeles-based artist whose work uses video, performance, and digital culture to explore how Black identity is shaped, performed, and circulated in today’s media-saturated world. Growing up in L.A. with a creative family, she went on to study film and new media, later co-founding a community art space in Chicago before returning home to launch Dominica Publishing – a press dedicated to examining Black visual culture. Syms blends humor, pop culture, and thoughtful theory in projects that mix personal memories, sitcom tropes, online clips, and archival material to ask how we learn to see ourselves in a world built on borrowed images and stereotypes. She shows that art and design are more than aesthetics – they’re forms of storytelling, identity-making, and critique. By revealing the systems that shape visibility and representation online, Syms encourages others to think more intelligently about who they’re creating for, why representation matters, and how art can make space for voices that are too often overlooked.
